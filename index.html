	    My body exhales and becomes dust again, but it is useless, just dust, unloved and never seen by lust
   
	Not for my faults, not for the indolent hand of God, but you.
	
	    You.
	    
You have forced this hand to numeric justice, you have cast my body in tarnished goods,
There is no lustre to my skin.
The soft pink flowers and summer birds shunned me,
In their shadow I floundered.
The soft tendrils of the buds were daggers that dared to scorn me

	
Can you blame me? 
When you have lied and maimed my stainless vessel. 
Have I not shown virtue?
You don’t understand, you.
All of you, 
Your minds are captured by men less worthy than I.
You know, their bones are not mended with gold.
Stupid creatures, digging shallow holes.



So now I exist here
	    

My body is all but numbers and characters.
